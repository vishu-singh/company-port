Frequently Asked Questions

General

Although a somewhat fluid term, a social enterprise is defined by the UK government as “a business with primarily social objectives whose surpluses are principally reinvested for that purpose”. That’s a pretty good starting place. It’s also worth noting the three different models of social enterprise out there in terms of social impact and profit generation and you can find a great explainer here.
We are a social enterprise whose social impact is directly tied to its trading activity (a “model three” social enterprise). So, if the enterprise is performing well financially then it necessarily will be performing well from a social impact point of view. And vice versa. This means we never have to choose between social impact and sustaining the business.

We are a ‘community interest company’ (CIC) which means we have an ‘asset lock’ in place which is a legal clause ensuring the assets of ProPair can only ever be used to serve our social purpose (like a charity).

We are a company limited by guarantee (CLG). This is a structure most often used by non-profit organisations and means there are no shares or shareholders, so the company cannot be sold for private gain nor distribute dividends.
The key reasons, which all interconnect, are:
1. We want to be financially sustainable and being dependent on grant funding and/or donations fundamentally undermines that objective;
2. We want to be independent and remove (or mitigate) any incentives that run counter to our social mission;
3. We want to tie our social impact directly to our financial success;
4. We want to provide our service for free to disadvantaged young people, sustainably, and at scale; and
5. We want a ‘price signal’ in place to help us build a better service.
At this early stage in the social enterprise’s journey net profits are put back into the software to keep improving it as well as growing our amazing Advisor-base. We are providing our service for free to a limited number of disadvantaged young people from the outset. As we grow, we will be able to increase this number significantly. Long term (or once we reach an adequate size), we will donate 100% of our net profits to charities aligned with our mission. These charities are written can be seen in our legal constitution.
